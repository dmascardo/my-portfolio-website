Personal Web Portfolio (PWP) – Milestone 2

Student: Denise Rose

Content Strategy

My site will be a one‑page portfolio with three sections that match the wireframes below.

Welcome

Intro headline: “Hello. I'm Denise Rose — Full‑Stack Developer.” Subline describing core focus. A row of tech icons (CSS, JS, Python, Java). Right side: hero image. One‑click CTAs to “View Projects” and “Get in Touch.”

About Me

Short, scannable paragraph about background (WGU CS, CNM bootcamp), strengths, and personality. Beneath: small paragraph box for an easy‑to‑skim description for employers. Tone remains personable and casual but professional.

Projects & Certifications

Section title with social icons (GitHub, LinkedIn, Hackerrank/Badge). Tagline under title: “Real code, real impact — minus the bugs (mostly). Backed by skills so official, they come with badges.” Grid of 4–6 work cards with thumbnails. Each card lists title, role, tech icons, and two links (Demo / Code). Certifications appear alongside or below as badge tiles.

Now Loading: Your Next Hire

Recruiter‑focused contact panel. Left: compact contact form (Name, Email, Message). Right: witty blurb, a button, and quick social buttons. Button copy options: “Let’s Connect :)” or “Explore my code.”

Footer

Copyright and social icons repeated for quick access.
